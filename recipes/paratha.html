Paratha

Paratha is a popular Indian flatbread made from whole wheat flour, typically layered with ghee or oil and cooked on a griddle.

Ingredients:
  • Whole wheat flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
Steps:
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, salt, and water to form a soft dough.
  2. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls and roll each ball into a circle on a floured surface.
  3. Brush the rolled-out dough with oil or ghee and fold it into a triangle or square, folding the edges to form layers.
  4. Roll out the folded dough into a flatbread again.
  5. Heat a griddle or skillet over medium heat and cook the paratha on both sides until golden brown, brushing with oil or ghee as needed.
  6. Remove from the griddle and serve hot with your favorite curry or condiments.
